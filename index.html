About Me

I am a Computational Chemistry Undergraduate Researcher working in the Skaggs School of Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences under Dr. Ruben Abagyan. The Abagyan lab focuses on structure-based drug discovery and optimization for target identification and protein modeling with an emphasis on multi-target pharmacology. I write bash scripts to extract information from specific protein targets from the Protein Data Bank for multi-target pharmacology profiling. Presentation

Cerebrospinal Fluid Single-Cell RNA Sequencing ML Disease Classifier

Built at UCSD's undergraduate bioinformatics hackathon, this project involved web scraping 1,000+ single-cell RNA sequencing cerebrospinal fluid datasets from Gene Expression Omnibus. I merged the data into a high-dimensional matrix using the Anndata library, visualized cellular clusters with UMAP and Scanpy, and trained a scikit-learn random forest classifier that predicts patient disease states with ~70% accuracy.

Applied Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce dimensionality and reveal biologically meaningful groupings across sample clusters.
